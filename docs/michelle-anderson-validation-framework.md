# Michelle Anderson Validation Framework

Assumption: a former CCSD 46 school board candidate is available to review the system.

## Objectives

- Validate whether the OS would have made the campaign easier.
- Extract concrete tribal knowledge.
- Identify missing readiness warnings.
- Correct inaccurate assumptions about CCSD 46 politics and governance.
- Convert experience into reusable public knowledge cards.

## Interview workflow

### Pre-interview package

Send:

- dashboard link or local file
- README
- readiness assessment
- political terrain draft
- source index
- explicit privacy/redaction policy

### Interview 1: campaign story map

- Why did you run?
- When did you decide?
- What did you do first?
- What did you learn too late?
- What was emotionally/financially harder than expected?
- Who were the key stakeholders?
- What narratives shaped the race?

### Interview 2: module review

Walk through each dashboard module and ask:

- Is this useful?
- Is anything misleading?
- What is missing?
- Would a first-time candidate understand it?
- What should trigger “not ready yet”?

### Interview 3: knowledge-card validation

Review extracted lessons:

- approve as public
- approve with edits
- keep private
- needs corroboration
- remove

## Feedback integration

1. Log every comment as an issue.
2. Separate factual corrections from strategic opinions.
3. Update source-backed district intelligence.
4. Convert experience claims into knowledge cards.
5. Add readiness blockers when the same risk appears repeatedly.
6. Publish a change log.

## Validation success question

Ask directly:

> Would this have made your campaign easier?

Then ask:

> What would still have been missing on the worst week of the campaign?
