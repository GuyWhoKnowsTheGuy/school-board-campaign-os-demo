# Candidate Onboarding Workflow

## Phase 0: Do not run yet — understand the burden

1. Read the stewardship statement.
2. Complete readiness assessment.
3. Review family and financial readiness.
4. Review public scrutiny and stress readiness.
5. Identify non-negotiable constraints.
6. Receive recommendation: Not Ready, Exploratory, Viable With Risks, or Ready.

## Phase 1: Exploratory candidacy

1. Attend at least three board meetings.
2. Read one year of agendas, minutes, and board packets.
3. Build a district issue map from public sources.
4. Meet 15-25 community stakeholders.
5. Create a personal why statement and problem statement.
6. Identify campaign treasurer/compliance advisor.

## Phase 2: Pre-launch validation

1. Verify eligibility and filing rules.
2. Draft petition plan and circulator list.
3. Build family/time calendar.
4. Create preliminary budget.
5. Recruit core team: manager, treasurer, volunteer lead, comms lead, data/ops lead.
6. Stress-test likely attacks.
7. Decide run/no-run.

## Phase 3: Launch

1. File/prepare legal paperwork as required.
2. Launch website/social presence.
3. Publish source-backed platform.
4. Begin structured voter contact.
5. Start fundraising and endorsement plan.

## Phase 4: Execution

Weekly rhythm:

- Monday: metrics and task review
- Tuesday/Wednesday: outreach + stakeholder meetings
- Thursday: content/message release
- Weekend: canvassing/events
- Sunday: family/rest/reset and postmortem notes
