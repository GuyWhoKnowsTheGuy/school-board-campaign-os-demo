# Tribal Knowledge Framework

This is the most important component of the OS.

## Goal

Turn undocumented campaign knowledge into public reusable infrastructure while protecting private people and sensitive data.

## Knowledge capture sources

- Former candidates
- Campaign managers
- treasurers
- volunteers
- school board members
- parent leaders
- teachers/staff when appropriate
- journalists / public records
- public meeting observations
- campaign postmortems

## Interview prompts

- What do you wish you knew before running?
- What surprised you most?
- What did you underestimate?
- Where did you waste time?
- What actually moved votes?
- What did consultants know that you did not?
- What did incumbents understand that challengers learned too late?
- What were the hidden costs?
- What attacks or narratives emerged?
- What would you tell a first-time candidate to do 12 months out?

## Transformation pipeline

1. Raw interview notes.
2. Redaction pass.
3. Claim extraction.
4. Evidence/source tagging.
5. District-specific vs generalizable classification.
6. Severity and confidence scoring.
7. Candidate-facing knowledge card.
8. Review by interviewee if possible.
9. Publish to OS knowledge base.

## Knowledge card shape

- Title
- Lesson
- Why it matters
- When it appears
- Warning signs
- Recommended playbook
- Evidence/confidence
- Source/redaction status
- Generalizable? yes/no

## Privacy rules

Never publish private accusations, donor lists, voter files, harassment details, student/family information, confidential legal advice, or unreleased campaign strategy.
