# Open Source Governance Model

## Mission

Make campaign knowledge public so execution and stewardship become the differentiators, not insider access.

## License recommendation

- Code: MIT or Apache-2.0.
- Documentation/templates: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0.
- Data packs: CC BY 4.0 where based on public facts; stricter privacy rules for interview-derived content.

## Stewardship structure

- Maintainers: approve product/code changes.
- Civic/legal reviewers: review compliance-sensitive content.
- District stewards: maintain district-specific packs.
- Candidate alumni reviewers: validate tribal knowledge.
- Privacy reviewers: enforce redaction and anti-harm rules.

## Contribution model

- Public issues for feature requests and source corrections.
- District data packs via pull request.
- Tribal knowledge submissions through structured form with redaction flow.
- All legal/compliance claims require source links and verification status.

## Rules of the commons

- No doxxing.
- No private voter files.
- No defamatory claims.
- No fake testimonials or synthetic personas.
- No unlabeled AI-generated content presented as human experience.
- No partisan capture of the core OS.
- District packs may describe real political terrain, but must distinguish fact, interpretation, and unverified anecdote.

## Race to stewardship excellence

The OS should be public. Campaign execution, trust, relationships, and judgment remain the differentiator.
