# Candidate Readiness Assessment System

The readiness system is intentionally conservative. It should protect candidates, families, and communities from avoidable harm.

## Categories

### Mission readiness

- Why are you running?
- Is the mission bigger than personal grievance?
- Can you state a community problem without demonizing people?
- Can you distinguish board governance from district management?

### Family readiness

- Does your family understand the time cost?
- Are childcare, transportation, meals, and schedule disruptions planned?
- Are there clear boundaries for privacy and public scrutiny?

### Financial readiness

Mandatory questions:

- What budget range is realistic for this race?
- How much can be raised without damaging relationships?
- Can the candidate absorb transportation, childcare, printing, event, domain, software, and opportunity costs?
- Is employment stable enough to survive campaign disruption?
- Is there an emergency reserve?
- Is spouse/family financial readiness explicit?

### Professional readiness

- Employer policy check
- Schedule flexibility
- Conflict-of-interest review
- Reputation risk

### Stress readiness

- Social media attacks
- Public criticism
- Opposition narratives
- Community conflict
- Emotional resilience plan

### Support-system readiness

- Volunteer base
- Advisors
- Treasurer/compliance help
- Technology support
- Communications support

## Output

- `Not Ready`: major blocker or multiple red flags.
- `Exploratory`: candidate should learn and prepare, not launch.
- `Viable With Risks`: launch possible with mitigations.
- `Ready`: minimum viable campaign foundation exists.
